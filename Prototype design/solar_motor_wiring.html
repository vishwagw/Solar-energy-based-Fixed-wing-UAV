üìã Step-by-Step Wiring Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Solar Panel Configuration
  • Series Connection (10V output):
  • Connect Solar Panel 1 POSITIVE ‚Üí Solar Panel 2 NEGATIVE
  • Use: Solar Panel 1 NEGATIVE and Solar Panel 2 POSITIVE as your main outputs
  • Wire gauge: 16-18 AWG for solar connections
Step 2: Power Management Bypass
  • Disconnect battery from power management unit
  • Solar Panel OUTPUT POSITIVE ‚Üí Power Management INPUT
  • Solar Panel OUTPUT NEGATIVE ‚Üí Power Management GROUND
  • Keep voltage regulation circuitry active
Step 3: ESC Connection
  • Power Management OUTPUT POSITIVE ‚Üí ESC POSITIVE (Red wire)
  • Power Management OUTPUT NEGATIVE ‚Üí ESC NEGATIVE (Black wire)
  • Arduino PWM Signal ‚Üí ESC Signal Wire (typically white/yellow)
  • Wire gauge: 14-16 AWG for motor power lines
Step 4: Motor Connection
  • ESC Motor Outputs ‚Üí Motor (3 wires for brushless)
  • No polarity concern for brushless motor
  • Secure all connections with proper connectors
Step 5: Control Signal
  • Arduino D3 (PWM) ‚Üí ESC Signal Wire
  • Arduino GND ‚Üí ESC Ground (signal ground)
  • Arduino powered separately (USB or separate 5V supply)

‚ö° Power Calculations

Expected Solar Output:
  • 2x 5V panels in series = 10V nominal
  • Estimated current per panel: 0.5-2A (depending on size/efficiency)
  • Total power available: 5-20 watts peak
  • Motor power requirement: 50-200 watts for normal operation
Reality Check:

Your solar panels will likely only provide enough power for very slow motor speeds. This is perfect for proving the concept but shows why battery backup is essential.

üß™ Test Procedure

Initial Setup:
  1. Mount solar panels facing bright light source
  2. Connect all wiring as per table above
  3. Set ESC to lowest throttle setting
  4. Have multimeter ready to monitor voltage/current
Power-On Test:
  1. Measure open-circuit voltage from solar panels
  2. Connect load and measure voltage under load
  3. Gradually increase ESC throttle
  4. Record motor response vs solar conditions
Variability Test:
  1. Cover/uncover panels to simulate clouds
  2. Change panel angle to sun
  3. Document motor speed variations
  4. Measure recovery time when light returns

üîß Required Components for Test

  • Wire: 16-18 AWG stranded for power, 22 AWG for signals
  • Connectors: XT30 or XT60 for power connections
  • Multimeter: For voltage/current monitoring
  • Breadboard jumpers: For temporary Arduino connections
  • Heat shrink tubing: For secure connections
  • Electrical tape: For insulation
